This Grievance Redressal Policy describes how Arovolife Private Limited (CIN U46909TS2026PTC210896) ("arovolife", "the Company") handles complaints from Distributors, customers, prospective Distributors, members of the public and third parties. It is published under Rule 12 of the Consumer Protection (Direct Selling) Rules 2021 ("DSR 2021"), the Consumer Protection Act 2019 and the Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023 ("DPDP Act").

If you have a complaint, please read §3 below for the fastest path to resolution.

## 2. Service-Level Commitments (SLA)

| Stage | Time-bound commitment |
|---|---|
| Acknowledgement of receipt (with ticket number) | within **48 hours** of receipt, by email and SMS |
| First substantive response | within **5 working days** |
| Resolution | within **30 days** of receipt for ordinary matters; up to **60 days** for matters that require a third party (banks, payment gateways, AUA/KUA partner, statutory authorities), with status updates every 15 days |
| Cooling-off refund | within **7 business days** of arovolife receiving the returned product, or within **7 business days** of cancellation for digital-only orders |
| Buyback refund (post-termination resaleable stock) | within **15 business days** of arovolife receiving the returned stock |
| Payout-failure investigation | within **5 working days** of the failed-payout notification |

Where arovolife cannot meet a stated SLA for reasons beyond its reasonable control, the complainant is notified in writing with the new expected resolution date and the cause of delay.

## 3. How to File a Grievance

You may raise a grievance through any of the following channels. Use whichever is most convenient — every channel routes into the same ticketing system.

### 3.1 In-App / Web Form (preferred)

1. Open your dashboard → Help → "Raise a Grievance" (Distributors), or
2. Visit `/contact-us` and choose reason "Grievance / Complaint" (anyone, including non-registered users), or
3. Visit `/p/grievance/form` once it is live in Phase 2.

You will be asked to describe the matter, attach evidence (PDFs, images, screenshots up to 10 MB each), and supply your contact details. On submission, a ticket number is issued immediately on screen, by email and by SMS.

### 3.2 Email

Write to `grievance@arovolife.in` *(TBD)*. The subject line should begin with "Grievance" and include any prior ticket number if applicable. Attach evidence. You will receive an automated acknowledgement with a ticket number within 48 hours.

### 3.3 Phone

Call the helpline number *(TBD)* during published hours. The agent will note your complaint, issue a ticket number, and send the confirmation by SMS.

### 3.4 Post

Send a signed written complaint to the Registered Office (address above). Postal complaints are scanned, time-stamped at receipt and routed to the same ticketing system. We recommend you send by registered/speed post and retain the proof of dispatch.

### 3.5 In Person

Walk-in at an authorised arovolife centre during business hours, where one is operational. The receiving officer issues a written acknowledgement with the ticket number.

## 5. What You Can Raise as a Grievance

Examples include (without limitation):

### Identity & KYC

- Registration rejected, stuck or delayed
- PAN / Aadhaar / bank verification errors
- KYC document mismatch / lost upload
- Account locked or suspended
- ADN issuance dispute
- Misuse of your identity to create a duplicate ADN

### Compensation & Payouts

- Missing or incorrect commission credit
- Failed payout to your bank account
- TDS discrepancies (Form 16A vs Form 26AS)
- Rank assignment / rank loss dispute
- Pool participation / qualification dispute
- Cap-related write-offs

### Genealogy & Placement

- Wrong sponsor recorded
- Wrong placement (leg, parent)
- Line-change request not honoured
- Suspected ghost / fake placements in your downline
- Slot squatting complaints
- Re-parenting of your downline after an upline termination

### Commerce, Returns & Refunds

- Product not delivered / damaged / wrong item
- Refund not received within SLA
- Cooling-off cancellation not honoured
- Buyback refused
- Invoice / GST error
- Daily order cap incorrectly enforced

### Ethics & Fraud

- A Distributor or admin breaching the Code of Ethics
- Unauthorised product claims
- Income projections by a third party
- Pressure-selling / harassment
- Listing of arovolife products on third-party marketplaces
- Bribe / favour demanded by an admin or trainer

### Privacy & Data

- Misuse of your personal data
- Marketing messages after opt-out
- Request to access / correct / erase your data
- Suspected data breach
- Inaccurate Form 26AS / Form 16A

### Platform & Security

- Account hacked or shared without consent
- Bug causing data loss
- Suspected manipulation of BV / PV / commission engine
- Accessibility issues with the platform

If your concern does not fit any of the above, raise it anyway — the ticketing system has a free-text category.

## 6. Records & Confidentiality

1. **Ticket numbers.** Every grievance receives a unique alphanumeric ticket number on receipt. Use it in all correspondence.
2. **Retention.** Grievance records (the complaint, every internal note, every communication with the complainant, the final order) are retained for **seven (7) years** from final closure, in accordance with DSR 2021 Rule 12.
3. **Confidentiality.** Personal information disclosed in a grievance is processed only for the purpose of investigating and resolving that grievance and (where required) for statutory reporting. It is shared only with the officers, vendors and authorities who have a need to know. No marketing use is made of grievance data.
4. **Anti-retaliation.** A Distributor who files a good-faith grievance is protected from any retaliatory action by their upline, downline or any administrator. Retaliation is a terminable breach of the Code of Ethics.
5. **Anonymous complaints.** Anonymous complaints are accepted (especially for ethics matters) but resolution feedback cannot be provided to an anonymous complainant. Internally, anonymous reports are investigated to the extent the evidence permits.
6. **Audit.** A quarterly internal audit reviews ticket volumes, response times, root causes and repeat issues. The summary is shared with the Compliance Committee and feeds into platform and policy improvements.

## 7. Mediation & Ombudsman

Where a grievance is closed at Step 4 (Compliance Committee) but the complainant remains dissatisfied, the following options are available **before** approaching a consumer forum:

1. **Mediation.** Within 30 days of the Step 4 order, the complainant may request mediation by a sole mediator. arovolife shall propose three mediators from the panel of the Bangalore International Mediation Centre or a recognised Indian alternative; the complainant chooses one. Mediation is conducted online (or in Hyderabad if both parties prefer). Costs are borne equally; arovolife waives its share where the complainant is a consumer or a Distributor with annual commission income below ₹3,00,000.
2. **Ombudsman scheme.** Where a self-regulatory ombudsman scheme for direct-selling entities is constituted by the Government or by an industry body of which arovolife is a member, the complainant may approach that ombudsman. arovolife will abide by the ombudsman's decision.

Mediation and ombudsman steps are voluntary and do not affect the statutory right of a consumer to approach a Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission directly.

## 8. Jurisdictional Courts

Subject to the arbitration clause in §15 of the Direct Seller Agreement and to the consumer's statutory right to approach a Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission of their own choosing, the courts at **Hyderabad, Telangana** have exclusive jurisdiction over any dispute arising from a grievance.

For DPDP Act complaints, jurisdiction lies with the Data Protection Board of India and its successors as constituted under that Act.
