## 3. Lawful Basis for Processing

Each category of processing is grounded in one or more of the lawful bases recognised by the DPDP Act:

- **Consent** — for registration, marketing communications, optional analytics, retention of supporting documents beyond statutory minima. Consent is captured electronically, version-stamped, and revocable.
- **Performance of contract** — for issuing your ADN, executing your orders, paying your commissions, processing returns and refunds, providing customer and Distributor support.
- **Legal obligation** — for KYC under DSR 2021; for TDS reporting under the Income Tax Act; for GST invoicing under the CGST Act; for record retention under the Companies Act 2013, the Income Tax Act §44AA, and DSR 2021 Rule 12; for grievance redressal under DSR 2021 Rule 12.
- **Legitimate use** under DPDP Act §7 — for fraud prevention, security incident response, internal audit and ensuring the integrity of the binary tree.

Where consent is the basis, you can withdraw at any time (see §10). Withdrawal does not affect the legality of processing performed before the withdrawal.

## 4. Purposes of Processing

We process your personal data for:

1. **Registration** — verifying your identity, age, eligibility; issuing your ADN; recording your sponsor and placement; capturing your consent log.
2. **KYC and re-KYC** — meeting statutory KYC obligations and verifying material changes.

8. **Platform improvement** — first-party analytics on aggregate, pseudonymised usage; bug diagnostics; performance tuning.
9. **Marketing** (only with consent and only after Phase 4 when the marketing module ships) — sending Distributor-only programme updates, product launches and ethics reminders.

We do not use your data for any other purpose without obtaining fresh consent.

## 6. Storage & Security

### 6.1 Encryption and the special case of Aadhaar and PAN

- **Aadhaar.** The full 12-digit Aadhaar number is held only in memory and only for the duration of the verification call to our UIDAI-approved AUA/KUA partner. The partner returns a verification reference token; the full Aadhaar is then destroyed. arovolife stores **only the reference token and the last 4 digits**. *(Hard rule #8.)*
- **PAN.** The full PAN is hashed with SHA-256; the platform stores the hash for uniqueness enforcement and the last 4 characters for display. The full PAN is also retained on the original uploaded PAN-card image (encrypted at rest in object storage) for the statutory retention period.
- **Bank account.** The full account number is encrypted at rest using a key managed by the platform's KMS; only the last 4 digits are shown in the UI.
- **Passwords.** Hashed using bcrypt (work factor ≥ 12). Never stored or logged in plain.
- **OTPs.** Stored hashed, with a short TTL, and rate-limited. Never logged.
- **Documents.** Stored in encrypted object storage in the India region; access requires authenticated, audit-logged, pre-signed URLs with short TTLs.

### 6.2 Transport security

All communication between your browser/app and arovolife is over TLS 1.2 or higher with modern ciphers and HSTS. Internal service-to-service calls run on a private network with mutual TLS where supported.

### 6.3 Access control

Access to personal data inside arovolife is on the principle of least privilege. Admin roles enforce separation of duties (e.g. an admin-finance user cannot freeze an account; an admin-compliance user cannot approve payouts). Every admin action that touches personal or financial data is recorded in an immutable audit log.

### 6.4 Logging hygiene

Application logs are scrubbed of personal identifiers. PAN, Aadhaar, OTPs, passwords, tokens and bank account numbers are never logged.

### 6.5 Breach notification

In the event of a personal-data breach, arovolife will:

1. notify the Data Protection Board of India within the timelines required by the DPDP Act and its rules;
2. notify each affected Data Principal in plain language describing what happened, what data was involved, what we are doing about it and what you can do; and
3. publish a public incident report after remediation.

## 10. Your Rights as a Data Principal

Under the DPDP Act and adjacent statutes you have the following rights, exercisable through `dpo@arovolife.in` *(TBD)*:

### 10.1 Right to information

You may ask what personal data we hold about you, the purposes for which we process it, the categories of recipients with whom it has been shared, and the retention periods.

### 10.2 Right to correction

You may request correction of inaccurate or out-of-date data. Some fields (PAN, Aadhaar reference, date of birth) can be corrected only with supporting documentary evidence because of KYC obligations.

### 10.3 Right to erasure

You may request erasure of personal data that is no longer necessary for the purpose for which it was collected or for which consent has been withdrawn. Data retained under a legal obligation (e.g. tax records within the 8-year window, grievance records within the 7-year window) cannot be erased on request; we will tell you which fields are blocked and why.

### 10.4 Right to data portability

You may request a structured, commonly used, machine-readable copy of the personal data you provided to us. Delivered within 30 days; large exports may be split.

### 10.5 Right to withdraw consent

Where processing is based on consent, you may withdraw it at any time. The withdrawal does not invalidate processing that occurred before the withdrawal. Some withdrawals (e.g. withdrawal of consent for KYC) may make it impossible for us to continue providing the service; in that case the ADN is terminated under the Direct Seller Agreement.

### 10.6 Right to nominate

Under DPDP Act §14, you may nominate another individual to exercise these rights in the event of your death or incapacity. Email `dpo@arovolife.in` *(TBD)* with the nominee's name and contact details; we will issue a nomination acknowledgement.

### 10.7 Right to grievance redressal

If you are not satisfied with our handling of any of the above rights, you may follow the escalation matrix at `/p/grievance` and, ultimately, approach the Data Protection Board of India.

We respond to verified rights requests within **30 days** of receipt. We may extend by a further 30 days for complex requests, with notice to you.
